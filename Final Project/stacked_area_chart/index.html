About this visualization

Global CO₂ Emissions by Source Over Time

This visualization shows the historical evolution of global CO₂ emissions broken down by major sources since 1750, capturing the dramatic changes in how human activities have impacted atmospheric CO₂ levels.

What does this chart show?

  • Displays annual CO₂ emissions from six major sources: coal, oil, gas, cement production, flaring, and other industrial processes
  • Measured in tonnes of CO₂
  • Based on territorial emissions (production-based)
  • Excludes emissions from land-use change and international aviation/shipping

Key insights:

  • The Industrial Revolution (late 1700s) marked the beginning of significant fossil fuel-based emissions, primarily from coal
  • Oil and gas emerged as major sources during the 20th century, diversifying our energy mix
  • Post-1950 shows dramatic acceleration in emissions across all sources
  • Recent years show continued dominance of fossil fuels despite growing climate concerns

Historical Context and Trends:

  • 1750-1850: Early Industrial Revolution - Coal dominates, marking the beginning of significant human-caused CO₂ emissions
  • 1850-1950: Industrial Expansion - Diversification of energy sources with the introduction of oil, steady increase in emissions
  • 1950-2000: Post-War Boom - Dramatic acceleration in emissions, with significant growth in oil and gas usage
  • 2000-Present: Modern Era - Continued growth in emissions despite environmental awareness, with notable increases in gas consumption and industrial processes
Source: Global Carbon Budget (2024)
Note: Emissions from international aviation and shipping are not included in national totals. The data represents territorial emissions and does not account for embedded emissions in traded goods.
