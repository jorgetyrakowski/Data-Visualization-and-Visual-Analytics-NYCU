About this visualization

CO₂ Emissions per GDP: Economic Efficiency in Carbon Output

This visualization shows the relationship between CO₂ emissions and economic output (GDP) across different countries, helping to identify which economies are more carbon-efficient in their economic activities.

What does this map show?

  • Displays CO₂ emissions per unit of GDP for each country
  • Darker colors indicate higher carbon intensity per economic output
  • Allows temporal comparison from 1800 to present
  • Highlights economic efficiency in terms of carbon emissions

Key insights:

  • Helps identify economies that are more carbon-efficient
  • Shows the evolution of carbon efficiency over time
  • Reveals regional patterns in economic carbon intensity
  • Indicates progress in decoupling growth from emissions
Source: Our World in Data (2024)
Note: GDP values are adjusted for inflation and purchasing power parity (PPP). Countries with no data for a given year are shown in gray.
