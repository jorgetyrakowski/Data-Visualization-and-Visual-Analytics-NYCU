Air Pollution in Seoul

About the Air Pollution Data
About the Data:

This visualization shows air pollution measurements from Seoul, South Korea. Data is collected hourly and aggregated into daily summaries.

Pollutants Measured:
  • SO₂ (Sulfur Dioxide): Industrial emissions and fuel combustion [Unit: ppm]
  • NO₂ (Nitrogen Dioxide): Vehicle emissions and industrial processes [Unit: ppm]
  • O₃ (Ozone): Created by chemical reactions between other pollutants [Unit: ppm]
  • CO (Carbon Monoxide): Vehicle emissions and incomplete combustion [Unit: ppm]
  • PM10 (Particulate Matter ≤10μm): Dust, smoke, and other particles [Unit: μg/m³]
  • PM2.5 (Particulate Matter ≤2.5μm): Fine particles from various sources [Unit: μg/m³]
Visualization Details:
  • Each row represents a different monitoring station
  • Color intensity indicates pollution levels (darker = higher concentration)
  • Hover over the chart to see detailed daily measurements
  • Switch between mean and median aggregations for daily values
Standard Ranges:
  • SO₂: 0-0.012 ppm (Standard range)
  • NO₂: 0-0.06 ppm
  • O₃: 0-0.07 ppm
  • CO: 0-1.5 ppm
  • PM10: 0-200 μg/m³
  • PM2.5: 0-80 μg/m³
